This chapter's examples

Cloud Seeding

c0301 <- sleuth.read('ascii/case0301.csv')
On 52 days, clouds were either seeded or not (randomly decided), and rainfall was recorded. There were an equal number of days in each treatment group. 
table(c0301$treatment)
## 
##   SEEDED UNSEEDED 
##       26       26

The data are skewed:

qplot(treatment, rainfall, data=c0301, geom='point') + coord_flip()

A log transform seems to give normally-distributed data of approximately equal spreads:

qplot(treatment, log(rainfall), data=c0301, geom='boxplot') + geom_point() +
   coord_flip()
qplot(log(rainfall), data=c0301, binwidth=1) + facet_grid(facets=treatment ~ .)
The log-transformed data fulfills the assumptions of a t-test: 
(tt <- t.test(log(rainfall) ~ treatment, data=c0301, var.equal=T))
## 
##  Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  log(rainfall) by treatment
## t = 2.544, df = 50, p-value = 0.01408
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  0.2409 2.0467
## sample estimates:
##   mean in group SEEDED mean in group UNSEEDED 
##                  5.134                  3.990

diff <- unname(tt$estimate[1] - tt$estimate[2])

The additive effect on log(rainfall) (from UNSEEDED to SEEDED) is 1.1438, so the multiplicative effect on rainfall itself is 3.1386. The 95% confidence interval for the ratio of SEEDED to UNSEEDED is (1.2723, 7.7423).

20 Means, Medians, Logs, Ratios

Here is some made-up data on college tuition:

ex0320 <- data.frame(college=c('a','b','c','d','e'),
                     outofstate=c(3000,8000,30000,32000,40000),
                     instate=c(1000,4000,5000,8000,40000))
ex0320
##   college outofstate instate
## 1       a       3000    1000
## 2       b       8000    4000
## 3       c      30000    5000
## 4       d      32000    8000
## 5       e      40000   40000

a) The average in-state tuition is 1.16 × 104. The average of log-instate is 8.6606. The log of the average instate is 9.3588.

b) These two are the same: 
median(log(ex0320$instate))
## [1] 8.517

log(median(ex0320$instate))
## [1] 8.517

The median of the ratios is not the same as the ratio of the medians:

median(ex0320$outofstate / ex0320$instate)
## [1] 3

median(ex0320$outofstate) / median(ex0320$instate)
## [1] 6

ex0321 <- read.csv('ascii/ex0321.csv')

The study asks whether umpires live shorter than expected. 32 of the umpires in the data had not died at the time of the study, so their lifelength was not known. The original study 'censored' their data. A t-test on non-censored data looks like this:

with(ex0321[ ex0321$censored == 0,], t.test(expected - lifelength, var.equal=T))

## alternative hypothesis: true mean is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  -0.5375  1.6144
## sample estimates:
## mean of x 
##    0.5385

30. Cloud seeding: multiplicative vs. additive effects

ex0330 <- read.csv('ascii/case0301.csv')
names(ex0330) <- tolower(names(ex0330))

Adding 100, 200, 300, and 400 to the unseeded rainfalls, and plotting the result:

us <- with(ex0330, rainfall[ treatment == 'UNSEEDED' ])
require(reshape2)
additive <- melt(data.frame(a=us, b=us+100, c=us+200, d=us+300, e=us+400),
                 variable.name='group',
                 value.name='rainfall')
## No id variables; using all as measure variables

qplot(group, rainfall, data=additive, geom=c('boxplot'))

Multiplying unseeded rainfalls by 2, 3, 4, and 5:

mult <- melt(data.frame(a=us, b=us*2, c=us*3, d=us*4, e=us*5),
                 variable.name='group',
                 value.name='rainfall')
## No id variables; using all as measure variables

qplot(group, rainfall, data=mult, geom=c('boxplot'))

The original data:

qplot(treatment, rainfall, data=ex0330, geom='boxplot')

Viewed in the context of these graphs, the effect 'looks' multiplicative.
